He checks signals like Google autocomplete and first-page SERP composition to verify there is active search demand and rankability.

He emphasizes that keyword prompts alone are not enough. To differentiate from generic AI content, he feeds in unique inputs such as:

- Unique workflows
- Distinct opinions or angles
- Topic-specific tutorials with firsthand details

He argues this uniqueness is the core ranking advantage over generic AI-only outputs.

In his recap, he says Claude Code is connected to Netlify so publishing is automated end-to-end.

## Key Takeaways
- The core strategy is not "AI writes everything"; it is AI plus unique input (case studies, firsthand workflows, and distinct angles) to create content that stands out.
- Start with keyword opportunities tied to content you already have, then validate demand and feasibility with autocomplete and SERP checks before writing.
- Prioritize low-friction ranking opportunities where first-page results are weaker or mismatched to user intent, then publish better-structured pages.
- Build repeatable prompts and templates for multi-post production, but keep each article differentiated with original evidence and examples.
- Treat Claude Code as an execution engine: planning, drafting, formatting, and publishing become a system rather than one-off tasks.
- Speed matters: fast creation and publishing cycles can capture early ranking opportunities, especially for emerging long-tail topics.
- For sustainable results, pair automation with quality control: keyword relevance, intent match, readability, and genuinely useful insights.
- The broader play is operational leverage: less manual publishing overhead, faster experimentation, and more consistent SEO output at scale.
