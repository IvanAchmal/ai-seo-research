# 6 AI SEO Skills That Will Matter Most in 2026  
Author: Nathan Gotch  
Link: https://youtu.be/mbCfRlY7elM?si=i8L4Q-XorwT-lR2L  
Date: 2026 

## Transcript
### Opening thesis
Nathan opens by saying six AI SEO skills can create a major business gap in outcomes, and that teams who adopt these skills early will scale faster than those using old SEO workflows alone.

### Skill 1: Prove AI SEO impact with clear measurement
He says AI SEO must be measurable to be sellable and scalable, and outlines four priority metrics.

Key signals highlighted:
- Market-weighted coverage (with heavier weight on Google and ChatGPT)
- Position inside answers (aim for top 3, not just "present")
- Share of voice (coverage + position + competitive context)
- Brand presence in citations/retrieval sources

He also recommends collecting a meaningful citation sample (roughly hundreds of citations) to find recurring sources and guide off-site strategy.

### Skill 2: Control brand narrative across trusted sources
Nathan says AI largely repeats what it finds online, so brand narrative must be managed across the platforms AI systems repeatedly cite.

Recommended focus:
- Identify the 5-10 most cited platforms in your niche
- Optimize profile copy for core topics/keywords (not just "complete" profiles)
- Build review diversity on niche-relevant platforms
- Keep brand details consistent across all major profiles and website assets

He adds that branded query pages on your own site (pricing, positioning, proof, FAQs) reduce reliance on third-party interpretation.

### Skill 3: Build a reusable knowledge base
He calls the knowledge base the "lead domino" for campaigns because it improves both content quality and high-leverage communication work.

Typical knowledge-base inputs:
- Positioning statements and messaging
- FAQs and objections
- Case studies and proof points
- Customer language and terminology

He suggests using project-based AI workspaces and tools that can ingest docs, URLs, reviews, and transcripts so outputs stay accurate and on brand.

### Skill 4: Create AI-assisted content with original value
Nathan warns against publishing auto-generated generic pages, and recommends combining knowledge-base context with optimization workflows before human QA.

Examples of differentiators:
- Original data
- Real screenshots
- First-hand experiences
- Documented workflows and expert interpretation

His filter: if AI can easily generate it, it is not enough to publish without added first-party value.

### Skill 5: Evolve link building into "citation source" strategy
He reframes classic link building into brand-mention engineering on sources AI already retrieves.

Execution approach:
- Identify top retrieval/citation sources for commercial queries
- Get repeated brand mentions on those sources
- Build your own site's authority so it can become a retrieval source too

He notes that influence can appear even with nofollow or unlinked mentions, so repeated presence in trusted source sets matters most.

### Skill 6: Develop practical AI coding leverage
Nathan positions AI coding as the highest-leverage skill: faster technical SEO fixes, faster architecture updates, faster launch cycles, and fewer dependency bottlenecks.

### Closing message
His closing message is that small teams can now compete with larger incumbents if they combine AI SEO strategy with practical building capability.

## Key Takeaways
- Track AI SEO like a channel: market-weighted coverage, answer position, share of voice, and citation-source presence.
- Prioritize visibility on platforms AI systems trust most; consistency and review diversity shape brand narrative in AI outputs.
- Build a central knowledge base first so AI-assisted content and communications stay accurate, fast, and on brand.
- Do not publish generic AI drafts; combine AI speed with first-party evidence and expert QA.
- Shift from backlinks-only thinking to repeated brand mentions on high-trust retrieval sources.
- Treat AI coding as a core SEO advantage for faster technical execution and experimentation.
- The compounding edge comes from systems: measurement + narrative control + differentiated assets + execution speed.
