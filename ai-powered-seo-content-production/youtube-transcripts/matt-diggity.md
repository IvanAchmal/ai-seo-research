### Core strategy: "Don't look like a marketer"
Reddit has strict community norms. Aggressive promotion can get accounts downvoted, reported, or banned.

#### Step 1: Build a low-friction profile
- Keep profile minimal and non-promotional.
- Avoid sales-heavy bios, CTAs, and external links early on.
- Use a normal avatar (not just a company logo).

#### Step 2: Lurk for 7-14 days
Before posting, learn each subreddit:
- Join the subreddit.
- Read the rules.
- Watch what gets upvoted.
- Notice what gets removed.
- Learn how members communicate.

He also suggests using AI tools to quickly summarize subreddit rules and tone.

#### Step 3: Choose subreddits strategically
Use a two-tier model:
- Tier 1: Smaller niche communities to build karma and trust.
- Tier 2: Larger subreddits after your account has history.

Selection workflow:
- Search your topic on Reddit.
- Click Communities.
- Join 10-15 relevant subreddits.
- Narrow down to 3-5 where you can contribute naturally.

Pro tip: Check which Reddit threads already rank on page one for your target Google keywords. These are often the same threads/subreddits AI systems read.

#### Step 4: Comment on rising threads
Focus on posts with momentum (`Rising` or `Hot`) rather than low-traction posts.

Contribute where you can:
- Answer with expertise.
- Correct misconceptions respectfully.
- Add missing context.
- Share quick step-by-step advice.
- Provide real examples.

Early comments tend to get more visibility and upvotes.

#### Step 5: Write comments that earn upvotes
Keep comments direct, useful, and easy to scan.

Six comment styles he recommends:
- Straight shooter: direct answer + actionable steps.
- Missing piece: add one key detail the thread lacks.
- Story drop: first-hand experience and lessons learned.
- Proof point: stats, studies, datasets, screenshots.
- Mini playbook: practical checklist people can use now.
- Brand voice: used sparingly when your brand is naturally mentioned.

#### Step 6: Create threads that become "AI food"
After earning enough karma and credibility, publish useful posts such as:
- Case studies.
- Lessons learned and mistakes to avoid.
- Project breakdowns (what worked and what did not).
- Free templates/resources.

He emphasizes structured formatting (headings, bullets, numbered steps) to improve readability for both users and AI systems.

### Measurement framework
Track performance through:
- AI mentions/citations in AI tool responses.
- Referral traffic (noting Reddit can appear as direct/unassigned).
- Engagement signals (comments, upvotes, replies).

If subreddit rules allow, add UTM parameters for cleaner tracking.
